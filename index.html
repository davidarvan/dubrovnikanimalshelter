Nestled in the hills of Bosanka, the Žarkovica Animal Shelter provides care to hundreds of abandoned and homeless dogs within the Dubrovnik region. Government assistance is limited and the no-kill sanctuary runs almost entirely on donations and volunteers. Sandra has run the shelter for 13 years and, despite limited funds, the compassion and love she has put into the shelter is readily apparent through the friendly barks and wagging tails that greet you as you enter the sanctuary. With no running water or electricity, taking care of so many animals is an arduous task and there is a great need for help. Please consider donating your time or funds to our cause.

If you would like to donate supplies please email Sandra at marin.sambrailo@du.t-com.hr or call (+385) 091 591-8434. The shelter does not have Internet so if you require a quick response time it is best to meet Sandra on site.

Volunteer

Donate your time and play with pups

The Dubrovnik Animal Shelter is open 365 days a year and volunteers are always welcome. Our response time to emails and calls is limited since we do not have Internet on site so please do not hesitate to come during the hours below without prior notice. Once you arrive simply reach out to an on-site staff member and they will get you started.

Note: Please bring lots of water, cleaning wipes or sanitizer, snacks and clothes you don’t mind getting dirty - there is no running water on site.

Hours:

  • 9-11am Dog Walking
  • 8am-2pm Distribute water and food
  • 8am-7pm Cleaning and tick removal
  • Lots of love and pets wanted during any of the hours above.

Getting there

All help welcome.

The Dubrovnik Animal Shelter is located about 10 minutes from the city center at Žarkovica ulica, 20207, Bosanka, Croatia. If you are coming by bus take #17 from Pile to Bosanka and mention to bus driver you are going to Žarkovica fortress - he will tell you where to go from the stop. If you don't mind walking you can also take the scenic cable car and walk 30 minutes to the shelter.

HELP OUR DOGS

SPREAD THE WORD: The city of Dubrovnik is attempting to shut down the shelter and build a new location that houses less than a quarter of the dogs we currently care for. The city generates more than enough tourism to financially support all its dogs. We urgently need public support and new homes for our dogs, or most will face euthanasia. Please get in touch or come and see us if you can help.
