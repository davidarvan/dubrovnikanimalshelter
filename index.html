Welcome to the
Dubrovnik Animal Shelter

Nestled in the hills of Bosanka, the Žarkovica Animal Shelter provides care to hundreds of abandoned and homeless dogs within the Dubrovnik region. Government assistance is limited and the no-kill sanctuary runs almost entirely on donations and volunteers.

Sandra has run the shelter for 13 years and, despite limited funds, the compassion and love she has put into the shelter is readily apparent through the friendly barks and wagging tails that greet you as you enter the sanctuary.

With no running water or electricity, taking care of so many animals is an arduous task and there is a great need for help. Please consider donating your time or funds to our cause.

Funds go directly towards veterinary services, food, water and shelter for the animals.

If you would like to donate supplies please email Sandra at marin.sambrailo@du.t-com.hr or call (+385) 091 591-8434. The shelter does not have Internet so if you require a quick response time it is best to meet Sandra on site.

Volunteer

All help welcome.

The Dubrovnik Animal Shelter is open 365 days a year and volunteers are always welcome. Our response time to emails and calls is limited since we do not have Internet on site so please do not hesitate to come during the hours below without prior notice. Once you arrive simply reach out to an on-site staff member and they will get you started.

Note: Please bring lots of water, cleaning wipes or sanitizer, snacks and clothes you don’t mind getting dirty - there is no running water on site.

Hours:

  • 9-11am Dog Walking
  • 8am-2pm Distribute water and food
  • 8am-7pm Cleaning and tick removal
  • Lots of love and pets wanted during any of the hours above.

Adopt

There are hundreds of animals at the shelter looking for a place to call home. For adoptions, please come to the shelter or contact Sandra via email marin.sambrailo@du.t-com.hr or phone. (+385) 091 591-8434

If you see an animal that looks like a stray please do not remove them from the area before speaking with the locals. There are many animals with owners that may look like strays.
